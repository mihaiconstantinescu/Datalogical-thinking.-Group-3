How the data was generated/curated/cleaned

The dataset consists of a list of 54 books with copynumbers, author names, total loans, shelfcode, publisher, publishing year, and place of publication.

The dataset is based on how real data from library catalogues can look, where copy-number (usually called “exemplarnummer” in Swedish library context), publishing details, authors and book titles are always present if the cataloging has been done correctly. As the majority of our group members have a LIS background this data felt familiar to us and understanding how the data could be used in a real world context was natural.

The data in this dataset has been made up. Therefore the authors, titles, loans etc are not corresponding to anything in real life. The copy-number is also shorter than one might find in real life but works in this context. The inclusion of the shelf-code might seem confusing to someone who has never worked in a library before but this is important information to have as someone who is in the library space and needs to find a book.

We focused on adding values in all fields, removed content that was not needed and separated the authors first and lastnames into columns as to fit better with our psudeocode.
